Psalm 84

I have read this Psalm mentioned below a number of times and each time it reveals to me in a fresh way the magnificence and the incredible aura of our Almighty God.

I just cannot help it, for I need to read these verses constantly. I would like to memorise these verses so they become a form of prayer. They bring healing and a supernatural peace to my soul!

Psalm 84:1-2
1) How lovely is your dwelling place,
O LORD Almighty!
2) My soul yearns, even faints,
for the courts of the LORD ;
my heart and my flesh cry out
for the living God.

There is no other exciting, thrilling and wonderful place to be than to be near the Presence of God.

One can sense how the three disciples felt when they saw the aura and the magnificence of our Almighty God.

Matthew 17:1-3 The Transfiguration
After six days Jesus took with him Peter, James and John the brother of James, and led them up a high mountain by themselves. 2) There he was transfigured before them. His face shone like the sun, and his clothes became as white as the light.
3) Just then there appeared before them Moses and Elijah, talking with Jesus.
4) Peter said to Jesus, "Lord, it is good for us to be here. If you wish, I will put up three shelters--one for you, one for Moses and one for Elijah."
5) While he was still speaking, a bright cloud enveloped them, and a voice from the cloud said, "This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased. Listen to him!"

Psalm 42:1-2
1 As the deer pants for streams of water, so my soul pants for you, O God.
2 My soul thirsts for God, for the living God. When can I go and meet with God?

The hunger to get to know our Lord must be strong. I remember once sitting quietly in the local church in London, England, on 3rd April 1993 and my mind was distracted in wanting to follow my own ambitions and desires. I sensed the voice of Our Heavenly Father ministering to me and instructing me, “Why don’t you, Anton, pursue my Son and pursue Him intensely?"

From that day on I decided to pursue this instruction meticulously. I came back home and took every single banking text book I owned and all paraphernalia relating to my potential successful career. I placed them neatly in a black garbage bag and threw them away. From then henceforth my challenging spiritual journey commenced.

I sometimes ask myself what did the apostle Paul mean to say or express in this verse : 1 Corinthians 9 verse 24) Do you not know that in a race all the runners run, but only one gets the prize?
Run in such a way as to get the prize. 25) Everyone who competes in the games goes into strict training. They do it to get a crown that will not last, but we do it to get a crown that will last forever. 26) Therefore I do not run like someone running aimlessly; I do not fight like a boxer beating the air.
27) No, I strike a blow to my body and make it my slave so that after I have preached to others, I myself will not be disqualified for the prize.
