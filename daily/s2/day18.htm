On Saturday 18th September 2010, we attended the much needed Prayer Vigil at Hyde Park, London. Between 8 pm and 8-30 pm, darkness had fallen and most of the 80,000 congregants assembled were on their knees, heads bowed in solemn prayer on the grass in Hyde Park. In devotion and reverence this Hymn was sung, "Lead Kindly Light".

Ephesians 6:18
And pray in the Spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests. With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the saints.

Ephesians 6:13
Therefore put on God's complete armour, that you may be able to resist and stand your ground on the evil day [of danger], and, having done all [the crisis demands], to stand [firmly in your place].

There is an evil day of danger which unfortunately will afflict, visit and confront everyone on the face of this earth. No one will escape this evil day of visitation. The question is how do you confront this day?
The next 5 or 6 daily readings will address this issue.

I would like to cite a story of a day that happened to be a very sad day and how God the Father took care of me and ministered to me in a unique way.

On the 28th of August 1995 we took a train (British rail) to go to church, near Wimbledon, South London. The train passed a graveyard and I am sure I must have seen this graveyard before on our visits to this church, but on this occasion this particular scene was passing a relevant message to me. As the train moved along the track, behold, we passed another graveyard and this time the message was very clear. I sensed God The Holy Ghost was preparing me for my dear mum's demise. When we came back from church I was very aware what was going to await me for this day. That evening I decided to go for a camp meeting and Our Lord graciously favoured me to hear an anointed man from America, a man they refer to as the preaching machine, the Rev. T. D. Jakes himself. He preached his heart out and than he stopped and asked everyone to stand up. As I stood up he asked each one standing beside each other to start praying for their neighbour. A Nigerian lady whom I have never met before, laid her hands on me and began to pray for me. She began to pray in tongues quietly and than the tongue went up to a higher crescendo. The lady started praying louder and louder and violently. Some way or other this praying began to minister to my spirit. Only when I came back home I realised what the praying was all about.

For whilst the lady was praying for me, at that same precise time which was about 8-15pm, my mum was breathing her last breath in Malaysia. God the Holy Ghost used the praying in tongues to strengthen my spirit to receive this sad news. The Presence of God compassionately comforted me and my spirit remained strong for the whole day. The preaching of the Lord's Word and the praying in the Spirit kept me emotionally stable.

When I came back home and told her what happened to me at the camp meeting she was so blessed to hear how God The Holy Spirit ministered to me in a very special way!
