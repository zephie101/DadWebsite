Interesting submissions by the Defence Lawyers on the case regarding Facts and Intent.

In a blow to the prosecution the High Court has reduced the sentences of the six church leaders.

City Harvest Church's Pastor Kong Hee's eight-year jail term was reduced to three-and-a-half years and the others all had their sentences reduced.

This was a result of the defence making a very well-reasoned case that the six church leaders should have been convicted of a less serious offence.

There are various offences of criminal breach of trust under the Penal Code of Singapore.

The six had originally been convicted of the most serious offence of criminal breach of trust, known as the s409 offence. Two of the three judges in the High Court found that the six did not fall within the meaning of "agent" under s409 and should instead have been convicted of the less serious offence of criminal breach of trust (the s406 offence).

These judges also said that there were exceptional mitigating factors including the fact that the conspiracy involved no personal gain on the part of Pastor Kong and the other church leaders and they felt it was telling that a substantial proportion of the congregation continued to support Ms Ho Yeow Sun’s career even after the full facts were brought to light.

By way of background, criminal breach of trust is when a person has been entrusted with property (in this case church funds) and they misappropriate that property. The s409 offence applies where there is a criminal breach of trust by a public servant, banker, merchant, broker, attorney or agent. The judges in the High Court found that the church leaders could not fall within s409 because their relationship with the church is internal. A criminal breach of trust in an external relationship, such as when a person offers his services to the community at large and makes a living from those services, may fall within the more serious s409 offence (and professional agent relationships certainly will fall within the offence).

Following this decision of the High Court, Law and Home Affairs Minister K Shanmugam has said that the Attorney General's Chambers ("AGC") will be looking into the judgment as it considers that the decision of the High Court has significant implications beyond the current case, particularly around the meaning of "agent" in the s409 offence.

The AGC has therefore made a Criminal Reference to the Court of Appeal. Criminal References are rare and only filed under exceptional circumstances where a party to a criminal proceeding wishes to refer a question of law or public interest. The AGC has said that if the Court of Appeal agrees with its submissions, the prosecution intends to ask the court to reinstate the original convictions under s409. The decision in a Criminal Reference is final. Please pray mercy will be extended to one and all.
