Professional Journey

A Brief Profile: Dietitian Experienced in Both NHS and Private Sectors

Valerie graduated from Reading University with an honours degree in Zoology and then completed her dietetics training at the University of London. She rose to become Chief III Dietitian at Guy's Teaching Hospital in London. Whilst at Guy’s, she played a vital part in setting up a Clinical Nutrition Working Party which produced an Enteral Feeding Policy. She also trained about 12 Dietetic Students a year, many of whom now hold senior positions in various hospitals throughout the United Kingdom. She assisted in various research projects, one of which included the effect of a long-term fat-modified diet on serum lipoprotein levels of cholesterol and triglyceride in patients on home haemodialysis (published in Clinical Science 1981).

In 1991, Valerie pioneered the dietetic service at a Rehabilitation Hospital and a Hospice in West London. She also had experience as a Community Dietitian within a Home Support Team (which included HIV-positive clients). Valerie acted single-handedly as the Dietitian at the prestigious Princess Grace Hospital and the Harley Street Clinic. Whilst there, she took sole responsibility for both hospitals, which included 2 ITU units and a renal unit. The patients came from all over the world. She also wrote diet sheets for private consultant referrals and gained experience on the management side too.

Smoking Cessation and Training

Smoking Cessation at Hillview Surgery

Valerie Meilton is a Smoking Cessation Community Advisor. She underwent training with The Thelma Golding Centre of Ealing, Hammersmith, and Hounslow Smoking Cessation Service. We offer a word of thanks to Dr. Shergill of Hillview Surgery, Bilton Road, Perivale, Greenford, Middlesex, who encouraged Valerie to take up this venture.

At the initial visit, we explain the treatment process and give clients advice about possible medication. After discussing their smoking habits, we then take a carbon monoxide reading, which is repeated at every visit to assess progress. We help clients prepare for their individual quit day and see them once a week to offer encouragement. This encouragement is really necessary for someone who wants to give up smoking. It is very much a team effort: clients are referred by GPs or Practice Nurses, often from a new patient check; reception staff book the client for the six-week course and help in the smooth running of the clinics.

Family Legacy

B.P. Nicholas

Bastianpillai Paul Nicholas founded the Oriental Bank of Malaya Limited in 1936, becoming the only ethnic Tamil to acquire a banking license in British Colonial Malaya. A visionary entrepreneur, he overcame colonial hurdles to establish a lasting financial legacy, headquartered in Kuala Lumpur.

Benedict Ponniah

Benedict Ponniah, a Queen’s Scholar in 1934, achieved a Triple First in Law at St. John’s College, Cambridge. He topped the Ceylon Civil Service exam and was poised to become Under Secretary to the UN Secretary General before his untimely death in 1949 at age 34.

Paul Nicholas

Paul Nicholas, great-grandson of B.P. Nicholas, graduated with an Honours Degree in Mathematics from Christ’s College, Cambridge in 2005. He later earned an MSc with distinction from Imperial College London, funded by his employer, reflecting the family’s mathematical legacy.

Jannine Nicholas

Jannine Nicholas graduated with a BA (Hons) in Law from St. John’s College, Cambridge in 2012, achieving 96% in Economics and serving as President of The Winfield Society (2011-2012). Her academic excellence continues the family’s Cambridge tradition.
